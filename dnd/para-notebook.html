  • The Train Cars
  • The Gambling Car
  • The dark king - Any buy in > 10
  • Blackjack but with d10 (1s are 1 or 11)
  • If you beat the dealer you get 2x your bet
  • All dice are shown except the dealers second dice
  • Setup, everyone is dealt 2 dice
  • On your tun you can hit any number of times or hold
  • Once everyone has gone the dealer reveals
  • The dealer hits as long as their hand is <= 16
  • A group of 3 pirates playing tricksters dice - 20 buy in
  • All participating players will roll 5d6 dice and keep their roll hidden
  • The first player to cry out Aye starts
  • They bid a face value, and the number of die they think have that value (1s are wild)
  • The next player has to raise either the face value or the number of dice. Or they can challenge
  • If they challenge and win, the loser’s dice goes in the middle and they take 4 of their gold. If they lose they lose a dice and 4 gold.
  • Fate’s weal - Any buy in > 10
  • Call a number, or even, or odd
  • The dealer rolls a d20
  • If you called even or odd correctly you get 1.5x your bet
  • If you guess exactly right you get 3x your bet
  • Gyp - 10 gp buy in
  • Roll 2d6
  • On a 7, or 12 you win 1.5x your bet
  • You can then walk away or double your bet
  • If you double your bet and add a d6
  • If you fail to get 7 or 12 you lose everything
  • Thimbles - Any buy in > 10
  • The game is played by casting small stones into a marked ring
  • (The rules are totally arbitrary)
  • You can only be told the rules after you buy in. Roll a d20 to determine the player’s innate skill at the game.
  • 2-4 players are equipped with smashers. You use your smashers to gather thimbles. You gather a thimble by hitting a stone through the opponent’s smashing window. You close a smashing window by hitting two thimbles through the window thus gathering two stones. Stones may be gathered by other means as well, two points for square throw and three for a if you’re playing Golbarthian rules, but nobody plays by Golbarthian rules. For every square, a thimble

touches as it falls and you remove one the opposing player’s smashers from the game. You also gain four thimbles and ownership of the squares.

Thread

  • Thread is a magical substance produced by the Cortas, and used to power most modern magic.
  • All magic comes from the soul. Unknown to anyone the Cortas are unraveling innocent familiars to make Thread.

The Nature of Magic

  • Everyone is born with a soul that manifests in the physical world as they’re familiar. All magic derives from these souls.
  • Wizards perform arcane heart surgery breaking their souls and reconstructing them to become more powerful. Wizards familiars often appear like shattered glass.
  • Arcane Trickster Rogues are like wizards through study they are able to somewhat open their souls and let magic pass through more easily, but unlike wizards they do not completely reshape their souls merely opening them up a little bit.
  • Warlocks sell their souls and in return are loaned the magic of a being with a much greater more powerful soul than their own. Consequently warlocks go without familiars.
  • Witches bond closely with their souls and pull them further into the material plane drawing on their souls’ magic and the magic of other untethered souls to fuel their spells.
  • Paladins and Clerics give their families up to a higher power, and become part of that power in return. They still have their familiars, but they are now an extension of a greater being’s soul. On loan to their original owner until their death. Each person who worships a saint bolsters that saint’s power by amassing the number of souls it has control over. Oathbreakers who leave the church they have sworn themselves to irrevocably corrupt their familiar.
  • Druids and Rangers draw gently on the souls of all living things around them. However, druids are careful never to take more than they need, and never to take so much that they cause harm. Hypothetically a druid of evil intentions could make all the land wither around them as they sucked the magic out of it, but in practice anybody who would do that would never be instructed in the circles of druidry.
  • Sorcerers’ souls are naturally more powerful than other humans. Whether because of a magical ancestor or because of pure luck sorcerers familiars are usually larger than typical familiars.
  • Bards are a combination of arcane tricksters and sorcerers. Like arcane tricksters they may have somewhat opened their soul through study, and like sorcerers they can have some innate power though perhaps their bloodline connection is more distant.

The Coven of Strings

The Coven of Strings subscribes to a philosophy that all spiritual entities are
interconnected by the threads of fate and the woven bonds they made while
alive. They use this knowledge to barter with the spirits and delve deeper into
the spiritual realm that binds them, harnessing the secrets that govern the very
basis of spiritual existence and finding the connections between the physical
and spiritual world.
The Covenant of Strings, as a result of their bartering nature, is more
sympathetic to the spirits these witches bind. Witches of this covenant tend to
see spirits more as allies, collecting objects of importance from their spirits as a
means of re-summoning the same ones. They trust the spirits they bind enough
to allow their power to grow under their command with the purpose of sharing
this power
