Worldbuilding Notes

Premise

The Dimension Jumper campaign is unique in that it doesn't take place in just one world, but rather in two. Long ago, in a world-changing event called the Crash, the planes of Fusiongauge and Mag Draíocht collided. The campaign is set about 500 years after the Crash. Nowadays, skilled mages known as dimension jumpers facilitate travel between the planes. But there's a catch, the universal constants — while similar — aren't exactly the same across the two worlds. A quirk in the makeup of Fusiongauge makes nuclear fusion incredibly easy to achieve there. However, the same quirk makes magic impossible. Mag Draíocht has the opposite problem. Magic may be possible, but fusion isn't.

Dimension Jumper Class

Dimension jumpers are a support class able to open portals between the worlds at will. They can also imbue items and people with physics from either plane. Portals between worlds are somewhat common, but the ability to summon them at will is still coveted. It’s also not uncommon for large wealthy cities to employ dimension jumpers to keep their city running in both worlds.

Technicalities

  • When jumping you can’t appear in a wall. You’ll just be placed either to the left or the right of it.
  • If you run into a portal you can run out the other side, but there is no infinite momentum like in the video game Portal.
  • Bullets and arrows can pass through portals, but a fireball will fizzle out.

Jump

You can expend one spell slot as an action to create a small one way portal to the other world. The portal dissipates after transporting up to two people for a 1st level spell slot, plus one additional person for each spell level higher than first, to a maximum of five. You can also dismiss the portal as a bonus action.

At 6th level, you can cast jump as a bonus action.

Convoy

At 2nd level, you can spend 10 mins. creating a large two way portal to the other world. The portal dissipates after transporting up to five willing creatures. You can also dismiss the portal as a bonus action.

At 6th level, you can cast Convoy in combat by expanding a spell slot of 2nd level spell slot, and the creatures that you transport using convoy no longer have to be willing. Tho they do have to make a Con save against your spell save DC.

Mag Draíocht /mag dry-oct/

Prehistory

Long ago, Danu, the mother goddess, gave birth to ten children who would later become the Tuatha dé Danann (or the children of Danu). Some years later, the Tuatha dé Danann fled to the four outer cities to escape a great war. They learned the secrets of science, art, agriculture, craftsmanship, architecture, and — most importantly — magic and druidry. When they returned to Mag Draíocht, they brought a dark mist that eclipsed the sky for three days and three nights. They also brought with them four magic artifacts, one from each city.

Upon their return, the Tuatha dé Danann found the war still raging. The endless war had changed the people into a warlike race known as the firbolg. The Tuatha dé Danann offered the firbolg two choices: either 1) continue their never-ending war in the north and cede the south to the Tuatha dé Danann, and the remaining people not yet corrupted, or 2) face war with the Tuatha dé Danann. The firbolg, unsurprisingly, chose war. The battle raged for three days and three nights. The Tuatha dé Danann won, banishing the firbolg to a small northern province, but their victory came at a price. Nuada, King of the Tuatha dé Danann, lost his right arm in the battle. No longer fit to be king, Nuada stepped down, and Bress took his place.

Bress, however, was half Fomorian, and he made the people of Mag Draíocht into slaves. Nuada went into hiding, emerging after seven years with a new arm wrought of silver, crafted by Creidhne in secret. This earned him the epithet Nuada, of the Silver Arm. He re-took his place as the rightful king of the Tuatha dé Danann and banished Bress, who was never heard from again. Nuada then made a truce with the firbolg, who had become less warlike and taken up a pastoral life during their time in exile.

Races

Automatons

There was once an ambitious master engineer. Inspired by the titans, he sought to create life. The engineer gave their all to birth such a creation, until finally, using clockwork prosthetics, the scientist created fully sentient automatons. At first they were mindless machines, blindly following orders, but eventually the engineer discovered how to upload other creatures' consciousness to them. Once uploaded they'd ask questions and wonder if they too were living beings. No such answer was found, and the scientist disappeared in the wake of their creations. Most automatons nowadays remain mindless servants. However, some possess the minds of once living creatures.

Tabaxi

Gauger tabaxi wait to name children till after they’ve said their first word. Language is widely considered to be what separates people from animals and speech is associated with having asoul.

Warforged

Warforged are thought to be descended from the titans. Warforged speak a ancient language called Titan.

Dwarfs

Dwarfs are a magically gifted race often referred to as Spellweavers. They craft intricate magic items inlaid with complex Celtic knot work based on Kēlen. They worship the Trí dé Danann.

Fae

Noun: Fae
Adjective: Fey

Fae are like elves from the Forgotten Realms. They're based off fairies or fair folk from celtic mythology. The fae are said to be the direct descendants of the Tuatha dé Danann. Unlike the dwarfs who are skilled in the creation of magical items, the fae some innate magical abilities and they can cast spells. However, they are less magically talented than the dwarfs.

Firbolg

Firbolg were the dominant race before the return of the Tuatha dé Danann. After countless years at war among themselves, they lost to the Tuatha dé Danann and were relegated to a small northern province where they became less warlike and took up a pastoral life. Even after their treaty with the Tuatha dé Danann, the Firbolg were still discriminated against. Though discrimination diminished in the years following the Crash, many Draíans still distrust the Firbolg.

Hybrids

Hybrids are a race of magically and genetically modified humans and fae. To clarify, hybrids are not half-elves or half-fae. Their in-game stats are based loosely off Simic hybrids although, unlike Simic hybrids, they don’t have animal parts. Currently the only known source of hybrids are the Domhan labs on Coriolis.

Runes

All magic comes from somewhere. Warlocks channel magic from their patron, paladins channel magic from their god, sorcerers channel magic through their blood, and wizards take the innate magic of the world and focus it with their mind and an arcane focus.

Similarly runes must draw their power from somewhere. Runes are just like magical instructions executing a spell, but without power they can't cast anything. Most ancient runes were tied to strong sources of natural power: like a god or an extremely ancient oak tree. More modern runes draw their power from the innate magic of the world; this allows them to work anywhere on mdo, but it can limit their output. More powerful spells require hundreds of tiny power aggregation runes painstakingly inscribed across an object. Your tattoo seems to draw power from the world (this is odd because runes that draw power from the world is a recent discovery, but your runes look ancient), but no way would that be enough power for what you claim they can do. There must be something else at work there

The Fomori

/foam-or-ee/

Singular: Fomorian

Plural: Fomori

Etymology: "fo" means under and "mór" means great or big. So "Fomorian" roughly translates to "the great underworld ones" or "the underworld giants"

The Fomori represent the harmful or destructive powers of nature. They are personifications of chaos, darkness, death, blight, and drought. They exist in opposition to the Tuatha dé Danann who represent growth and civilization. The Fomori and the Tuatha dé Danann are descended from the sister goddesses Domnu and Danu respectively.

Domnu is the mother of the ancient race of the Fomori, older sister of Danu, who we meet at the Winter Solstice. Their story symbolises the struggle between light and shadow. Not much is known about either goddesses both in the campaign and in real life.

The Veil

The Veil is the space between the worlds. It’s less of a place and more of a space between places. If Fusiongauge and Mag Draíocht were adjacent rooms the Veil would be the doorway connecting them.

Jump Alloy

Scientific Name: Veilenium /veil-en-e-um/

Etymology: "Vale" from the Veil and "enium" is a suffix that simply means metal

"Two metals form an alloy, coming together to create a stronger whole. If you can make an alloy as strong as steel from something as simple as iron and carbon, imagine the properties of an alloy made from the collision of two planes!?"

Veilenium is more well known as Jump Alloy or just Alloy for short. Alloy is slightly softer than iron and has an iridescent look, not unlike real-world bismuth. Alloy is a critical component for dimension jumpers. Additionally, Fusion reactors made of Alloy and Spellweaver runes inscribed in Alloy work on both planes. Because of this and because it can only be found in the Doubled Peaks alloy is expensive and rare.

They get their name from the blurry mirror image of their counterpart mountain range visible through the Veil.
